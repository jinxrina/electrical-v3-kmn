Singapore Regulations Reference

According to Singapore's Code of Practice CP5:2018:

  • For residential installations, the maximum voltage drop should not exceed 2.5% of nominal voltage.
  • All cables must be sized to carry the design current while considering correction factors for ambient temperature and grouping.
  • Protective devices must be selected to protect against overload and short circuit conditions.

"The nominal cross-sectional area of conductors shall be not less than the values specified in SS 638." - CP5:2018 Section 5.1.2

Singapore Regulations Reference

According to Singapore's Code of Practice CP5:2018:

  • RCDs with a rated residual operating current not exceeding 30mA are required for socket outlets and lighting circuits in residential premises.
  • Selection of circuit breakers must consider both the load current and cable protection requirements.
  • MCB types (B, C, or D) should be selected based on the load characteristics.

"Every final socket-outlet circuit shall be protected by an RCD with a rated residual operating current not exceeding 30mA." - CP5:2018 Section 7.1.2

"The lighting in buildings shall be designed and installed to provide adequate illumination for the intended use of the building." - SS531

Electrical Fault Calculator

Fault Calculation Results

Max Thermal Stress on Cable: 24.5 kA² s
Minimum Breaking Capacity Required: 16 kA

Singapore Regulations Reference

According to Singapore's Code of Practice CP5:2018:

  • Protection devices must have adequate breaking capacity for the prospective short-circuit current at the point of installation.
  • Earth fault loop impedance must be low enough to allow sufficient fault current to flow to operate the protective device within the required time.
  • Conductors must be protected against thermal effects of short-circuits and faults.

"Every overcurrent protective device shall have a breaking capacity not less than the prospective short-circuit current at the point where the device is installed." - CP5:2018 Section 5.3.5

Singapore Electrical Regulations

All calculations in this tool are based on the following Singapore standards:

  • CP5:2018: Code of Practice for Electrical Installations
  • SS 638: Electrical cables - Cables for power and general applications
  • SS 531: Code of Practice for Lighting of Work Places
  • SS 530: Energy Efficiency Standard for Building Services and Equipment
  • SS CP 88: Code of Practice for Temporary Electrical Installations

These standards ensure that electrical installations in Singapore are safe, reliable, and efficient. Our calculations comply with the latest amendments and revisions of these standards.
